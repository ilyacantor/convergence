## 1. Revenue Recognition

### 1.1 Managed Services Revenue

- **Policy:** Managed services revenue is recognized ratably on a monthly basis over the contract term. Contracts carry a fixed monthly fee regardless of volume fluctuations within the contracted scope. Revenue recognition begins on the service commencement date (when Cascadia assumes operational responsibility for the contracted processes).
- **Scope:** All managed services contracts with fixed monthly fees. Represents approximately 44% of total revenue ($440M).
- **Boundary:** Does not apply to per-FTE or per-transaction revenue streams. Volume overages beyond the contracted scope are billed and recognized separately as per-transaction revenue.

### 1.2 Per-FTE Revenue

- **Policy:** Per-FTE revenue is recognized monthly based on the number of FTEs deployed to the client multiplied by the contracted monthly rate per FTE. FTE counts are reconciled monthly against deployment records. Cascadia books the full FTE rate as revenue — this is a COFA conflict with Meridian, which books contractor markup as revenue. The difference creates an approximately $50M delta in top-line revenue comparison between the two entities at comparable service levels.
- **Scope:** All per-FTE contracts. Represents approximately 37% of total revenue ($370M). Average FTEs per contract: 140.
- **Boundary:** Does not apply to managed services (fixed fee) or per-transaction revenue. Bench FTEs (between contracts) are not revenue-generating and their cost is in COGS.

### 1.3 Per-Transaction Revenue

- **Policy:** Per-transaction revenue is recognized as transactions are processed, based on actual transaction volumes multiplied by the contracted price per transaction. Volumes are tracked by the delivery platform and reconciled monthly. Where contracts include volume-based pricing tiers (higher volumes attract lower per-unit rates), variable consideration is estimated using the expected value method — Cascadia calculates the probability-weighted average across all tier outcomes based on historical volume patterns and current-period trends. Variable consideration is constrained to the extent that a significant revenue reversal is not probable.
- **Scope:** All per-transaction contracts. Represents approximately 19% of total revenue ($190M).
- **Boundary:** Does not apply to managed services or per-FTE revenue streams.

### 1.4 Service Line Distribution

- **Policy:** Revenue is tracked and reported by service line: Finance & Accounting 30% ($300M), Customer Operations 28% ($280M), HR Operations 25% ($250M), Supply Chain 17% ($170M).
- **Scope:** All revenue streams across all service lines.
- **Boundary:** Revenue is attributed to the service line that owns the contract. Cross-service-line engagements are attributed to the primary service line.

### 1.5 Contract Balances

- **Policy:** Deferred revenue is recorded when cash is received or invoiced in advance of performance. Starting deferred revenue reflects approximately 2.0 months of revenue, higher than Meridian (1.5 months) due to upfront billing on managed services contracts. Accounts receivable DSO is 52 days. Unbilled revenue is $28M (lower than Meridian's $340M because BPM contracts are typically billed in advance or concurrently, not in arrears).
- **Scope:** All customer contracts across all revenue streams.
- **Boundary:** Does not cover bad debt reserves (see Explicit Gaps).

## 2. Cost of Revenue (COGS)

- **Policy:** Cost of revenue is 71% of revenue ($710M on a $1B base) and consists of seven components:
  - **Offshore labor:** 43.8% of COGS (~$311M). Delivery staff in India and Philippines at ~$20K average compensation.
  - **Onshore labor:** 11.0% of COGS (~$78M). Delivery staff in United States and United Kingdom at ~$70K average compensation.
  - **Nearshore labor:** 11.0% of COGS (~$78M). Delivery staff in Costa Rica and Poland at ~$20K average compensation.
  - **Benefits:** 10.1% of COGS (~$72M). Employee benefits reported as a separate COGS line item. This is a COFA conflict with Meridian, which bundles benefits into consultant compensation as a single line. Gross margins are not directly comparable without reclassifying one entity's treatment to match the other.
  - **Bench delivery staff:** 9.6% of COGS (~$68M). Idle delivery personnel between contracts. Approximately 500 bench delivery FTEs.
  - **Delivery center operations:** 8.4% of COGS (~$60M). Facility costs for the 6 delivery centers (Mumbai, Manila, San Jose CR, Warsaw, Chicago, London).
  - **Subcontractors:** 6.1% of COGS (~$43M).
- **Scope:** All costs directly attributable to delivering BPM services.
- **Boundary:** Does not include operating expenses. Gross margin is 29% — substantially lower than Meridian's 39% due to the labor-intensive delivery model, but part of the gap is also the benefits-bundling COFA conflict.

## 3. Operating Expenses

- **Policy:** Operating expenses total approximately 43.2% of revenue ($432M) and are reported as follows:
  - **General & Administrative:** 16.5% of revenue ($165M). Larger corporate overhead relative to revenue than Meridian.
  - **Sales & Marketing (bundled):** 13.2% of revenue ($132M). Sales and Marketing are combined into a single line item. This is a COFA conflict with Meridian, which reports Sales (4%) and Marketing (2.5%) as separate lines. The combined entity cannot produce segment-level S&M comparisons without reclassification.
  - **Technology & Automation:** 8.0% of revenue ($80M). Partially capitalized — $12M/year of automation platform development is capitalized as intangible assets (see Capitalization Policy). The remaining $68M is expensed.
  - **Corporate Facilities:** 5.5% of revenue ($55M).
  - **Recruiting (partially capitalized):** 0.8% of revenue ($8M capitalized portion). Recruiting costs tied to new contract wins are capitalized. This is a COFA conflict with Meridian, which expenses all recruiting costs as incurred.
- **Scope:** All operating costs not included in COGS.
- **Boundary:** GP = $290M, OpEx ≈ $432M → Operating income is negative at this revenue scale. Growth trajectory (8% revenue growth) is expected to improve operating leverage over time.

## 4. Capitalization Policy

- **Policy:** Cascadia capitalizes two categories of expenditure that Meridian expenses:
  - **Recruiting costs:** $8M/year of recruiting costs directly tied to winning new BPM contracts are capitalized as contract acquisition costs and amortized over the average contract duration (3.5 years).
  - **Automation platform development:** $12M/year of internal automation platform development costs are capitalized as intangible assets. Starting intangible assets balance is $95M (includes capitalized software and automation). Amortization follows the accelerated depreciation schedule (3 years).
  Together, these represent $20M/year of costs that would be expensed under Meridian's treatment. This is the largest single driver of COFA non-comparability in operating expense lines.
- **Scope:** Recruiting costs specifically tied to new contract acquisition, and automation platform development costs past the preliminary project stage.
- **Boundary:** General recruiting costs (for backfill, corporate hiring) are expensed as incurred. Maintenance and minor enhancements to the automation platform are expensed as incurred.

## 5. Property and Equipment

### 5.1 Balance Sheet

- **Policy:** Property and equipment are recorded at historical cost. Starting PP&E balance is $185M, primarily delivery center equipment across 6 locations. Goodwill is $320M. Capital expenditures are 3.5% of revenue (~$35M/year), higher than Meridian's 1.5% due to delivery center equipment investment.
- **Scope:** Delivery center equipment, office equipment, leasehold improvements.
- **Boundary:** Does not include capitalized intangible assets ($95M, accounted for under Capitalization Policy). Does not include right-of-use lease assets.

### 5.2 Depreciation

- **Policy:** Delivery center equipment and capitalized intangibles are depreciated using the accelerated method over a 3-year useful life. This front-loads depreciation expense — higher expense in early years, lower in later years.
- **Scope:** All capitalized property, equipment, and intangible assets.
- **Boundary:** This is a COFA conflict with Meridian, which uses straight-line depreciation over 5 years. On equivalent asset values, Cascadia's treatment produces higher annual depreciation in years 1-2 and lower in years 3-5. Combined depreciation schedules are not comparable without harmonization.

## 6. Debt and Financing

- **Policy:** Long-term debt of $150M (delivery center financing, approximately 15% of revenue). Quarterly amortization at 2.5% of outstanding balance. No dividends — all earnings reinvested. Paid-in capital of $50M (compared to Meridian's $200M).
- **Scope:** All debt instruments.
- **Boundary:** Does not cover interest expense policy or debt covenants (see Explicit Gaps).

---

## 7. Tax

- **Policy:** Effective tax rate of 22%, lower than Meridian's 24% due to offshore operations (delivery centers in lower-tax jurisdictions).
- **Scope:** All taxable income.
- **Boundary:** This is a simplified rate assumption. No policy for deferred tax, uncertain tax positions, or transfer pricing (see Explicit Gaps).

---

## 8. Regional Operations

- **Policy:** Revenue is distributed across three regions: AMER 55% ($550M), EMEA 25% ($250M), APAC 20% ($200M). Delivery operations span 6 geographies: India, Philippines, Costa Rica, Poland, United States, United Kingdom.
- **Scope:** All revenue and operating metrics.
- **Boundary:** Revenue regions and delivery geos are not the same dimension — a contract may be AMER revenue but delivered from India. No foreign currency translation policy documented (see Explicit Gaps).

---

## 9. Headcount and People Costs

- **Policy:** Total headcount 8,000 (6,500 delivery W-2 employees + 1,500 corporate staff). Bench delivery FTEs: 500. Annual attrition: 22% for delivery staff, 10% for corporate. Cascadia counts W-2 employees only in headcount — this is a COFA conflict with Meridian, which includes contractors in headcount.
  Corporate staff distribution: Sales & Marketing 16.7% (bundled), Operations 20%, G&A 32.9%, Finance 11.7%, IT 9.3%, HR 6.7%, Legal 2.7%.
- **Scope:** All personnel and related costs.
- **Boundary:** Stock-based compensation is not documented and assumed immaterial for a bootstrapped company (see Explicit Gaps).

---

## 10. Delivery Metrics

- **Policy:** Cascadia tracks operational delivery metrics that have no equivalent in Meridian's consultancy model: total processes managed (850), automation rate (35%, improving ~3 percentage points per year), SLA attainment (97.2%). These metrics are used for internal performance measurement and client reporting but do not directly affect revenue recognition.
- **Scope:** All delivery operations across all service lines and delivery geos.
- **Boundary:** SLA attainment does not trigger revenue adjustments unless the contract includes performance penalties (accounted for as variable consideration constraints).

## 11. Intercompany Transactions

- **Policy:** Not applicable. Cascadia is a standalone entity prior to the Meridian acquisition.
- **Scope:** N/A.
- **Boundary:** Post-acquisition intercompany transactions with Meridian will require a separate elimination policy.

## Explicit Gaps

The following accounting policy areas are not documented for Cascadia and must be investigated before the agent may apply any treatment. Per Axiom 7 (No GAAP Inference), the absence of a policy means the agent must flag and halt — not guess.

| Gap | Reason | Risk if Unaddressed |
|-----|--------|---------------------|
| **Foreign currency** | Cascadia has delivery operations in 6 countries but no FX translation policy. Delivery center costs in INR, PHP, CRC, PLN are material. | Unreported FX gains/losses on labor costs could misstate COGS and operating income. |
| **Goodwill impairment** | $320M goodwill on the balance sheet. No impairment testing methodology or triggers documented. | Overstated goodwill if reporting units underperform. |
| **Bad debt / credit losses** | No allowance for doubtful accounts policy despite $144M starting AR. | Overstated receivables if customers default. |
| **Stock-based compensation** | Assumed immaterial (bootstrapped company) but not confirmed. Post-acquisition, acquirer equity grants to Cascadia employees would make this material. | Understated compensation expense if equity awards exist or are granted post-close. |
| **Interest expense** | $150M delivery center financing but no interest rate or expense recognition policy. | Missing interest expense in P&L. |
| **Lease accounting** | No ASC 842 policy despite 6 delivery centers and corporate offices. | Potentially unrecognized ROU assets and lease liabilities. |
| **Deferred tax** | 22% effective rate is a simplification. No deferred tax policy, no uncertain tax position policy, no transfer pricing policy for multi-geo delivery operations. | Materially misstated tax balances, especially given offshore operations. |
| **Cash-to-accrual conversion** | NLQ seed data indicates Cascadia is transitioning from cash basis (QuickBooks) to accrual basis post-acquisition. No conversion methodology documented. | Revenue and expense timing differences during transition could misstate period results. |
| **Impairment of long-lived assets** | $185M PP&E in delivery center equipment. No ASC 360 impairment testing policy. | Overstatement of PP&E if delivery centers are underutilized or closed. |
