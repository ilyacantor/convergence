## 1. Revenue Recognition

### 1.1 Time & Materials Revenue

- **Policy:** Time & materials revenue is recognized as services are delivered, based on hours worked multiplied by the contracted billing rate. Revenue is measured monthly using actual billable hours recorded in the PSA system (Kantata), multiplied by the client-specific billing rate. The blended average billing rate is $350/hour across all consultant levels. Utilization is tracked at 72% billable, with a 94% realization rate applied (ratio of collected to billed). Revenue is recognized net of realization — amounts billed but not expected to be collected are not recognized as revenue.
- **Scope:** All time & materials consulting engagements. Represents approximately 65% of total revenue.
- **Boundary:** Does not apply to fixed-fee project revenue. Unbilled hours (work performed but not yet invoiced) are recorded as contract assets (unbilled revenue / WIP).

### 1.2 Fixed-Fee Project Revenue

- **Policy:** Fixed-fee project revenue is recognized over time using the input method based on hours incurred relative to total estimated hours for the engagement. Progress is measured as cumulative hours incurred divided by total estimated hours at completion. Estimates are updated each reporting period; changes in estimates are recognized on a cumulative catch-up basis in the period of change. If a project is estimated to result in a loss, the entire estimated loss is recognized immediately in the period it becomes probable.
- **Scope:** All fixed-fee consulting engagements. Represents approximately 35% of total revenue.
- **Boundary:** Does not apply to time & materials revenue. Does not apply to reimbursable expenses billed to clients (passed through at cost, recognized as revenue when incurred).

### 1.3 Practice Area Revenue Distribution

- **Policy:** Revenue is tracked and reported by practice area. Revenue is attributed to the practice area that owns the engagement, regardless of which consultants are staffed. Practice area revenue distribution at baseline: Strategy 18%, Operations 22%, Technology 20%, Risk 12%, Digital/AI 16%, Commercial 12%.
- **Scope:** All revenue across both T&M and fixed-fee engagements.
- **Boundary:** Cross-practice engagements are attributed to the lead practice area. Revenue is not split across practice areas within a single engagement.

### 1.4 Contract Balances

- **Policy:** Unbilled revenue (WIP) is recorded when services have been delivered but not yet invoiced. Unbilled revenue represents a contract asset. Deferred revenue is recorded when cash is received or invoiced in advance of performance. Starting unbilled revenue balance is $340M, reflecting the consultancy pattern of billing in arrears. Starting deferred revenue is approximately 1.5 months of revenue, reflecting retainers and advance billings. Accounts receivable DSO is 58 days, reflecting the longer payment cycles typical for enterprise consulting.
- **Scope:** All customer contracts.
- **Boundary:** Does not cover bad debt reserves or credit losses (see Explicit Gaps).

## 2. Cost of Revenue (COGS)

- **Policy:** Cost of revenue is 61% of revenue ($3,050M on a $5B base) and consists of four components:
  - **Consultant compensation including benefits:** 62% of COGS (~$1,891M). Compensation and benefits are bundled into a single COGS line. This is a COFA conflict with Cascadia, which separates benefits from compensation as a distinct line.
  - **Bench cost:** 26.7% of COGS (~$814M). Cost of consultants not currently deployed to billable engagements. Approximately 4,500 bench consultants at $15,100/month each.
  - **Subcontractors:** 8.2% of COGS (~$250M). Third-party contractors engaged for client projects. Meridian books the contractor markup as revenue (not the full contractor rate). This is a COFA conflict with Cascadia, which books full FTE rates as revenue (~$50M delta in top-line comparison).
  - **Travel:** 3.1% of COGS (~$95M). Client-related travel expenses.
- **Scope:** All costs directly attributable to delivering consulting services.
- **Boundary:** Does not include operating expenses (Sales, Marketing, R&D, G&A, Facilities, Recruiting, D&A). The benefits-bundled treatment means Meridian's consultant compensation line is not directly comparable to Cascadia's labor lines without reclassification.

---

## 3. Operating Expenses

- **Policy:** Operating expenses are 17% of revenue ($850M) and reported as separate line items:
  - **Sales:** 4% of revenue ($200M). Sales is a standalone line item, separate from Marketing. This is a COFA conflict with Cascadia, which bundles Sales & Marketing into one line.
  - **Marketing:** 2.5% of revenue ($125M). Marketing is a standalone line item, separate from Sales.
  - **Research & Development:** 1.5% of revenue ($75M). Methodology and tools development. All R&D costs are expensed as incurred — Meridian does not capitalize any development costs. This is a COFA conflict with Cascadia, which capitalizes automation platform development ($12M/year).
  - **General & Administrative:** 5.5% of revenue ($275M).
  - **Depreciation & Amortization:** 2% of revenue ($100M). Straight-line method over 5-year useful life. This is a COFA conflict with Cascadia, which uses accelerated depreciation over 3 years.
  - **Facilities:** 0.5% of revenue ($25M).
  - **Recruiting:** 1% of revenue ($50M). All recruiting costs are expensed as incurred — Meridian does not capitalize any recruiting costs. This is a COFA conflict with Cascadia, which capitalizes $8M/year of recruiting tied to new contracts.
- **Scope:** All operating costs not included in COGS.
- **Boundary:** EBIT = Revenue - COGS - OpEx = $5,000M - $3,050M - $850M = $1,100M (22% margin).

## 4. Capitalization Policy

- **Policy:** Meridian expenses all recruiting costs and all technology/R&D costs as incurred. There are no capitalized contract acquisition costs and no capitalized internal development costs. Capital expenditures are limited to property and equipment (1.5% of revenue, ~$75M/year).
- **Scope:** All recruiting, R&D, and technology expenditures.
- **Boundary:** This is a COFA conflict on two dimensions: Cascadia capitalizes recruiting ($8M/year) and automation platform development ($12M/year). When comparing operating income between entities, Meridian's OpEx includes $20M+ of costs that would be capitalized under Cascadia's treatment.

## 5. Property and Equipment

### 5.1 Balance Sheet

- **Policy:** Property and equipment are recorded at historical cost. Starting PP&E balance is $420M. Intangible assets (purchased software, methodologies) are $180M. Goodwill from prior acquisitions is $2,400M.
- **Scope:** Office equipment, leasehold improvements, purchased software licenses, and goodwill.
- **Boundary:** Does not include right-of-use lease assets. Does not include internally developed software (Meridian does not capitalize internal development).

### 5.2 Depreciation

- **Policy:** All fixed assets are depreciated using the straight-line method over a 5-year useful life. This applies uniformly across all asset classes.
- **Scope:** All capitalized property and equipment.
- **Boundary:** This is a COFA conflict with Cascadia, which uses accelerated depreciation over 3 years on delivery center equipment. Straight-line 5-year produces lower annual depreciation expense but over a longer period. Combined entity depreciation schedules will not be directly comparable without harmonization.

## 6. Debt and Financing

- **Policy:** Long-term debt of $500M (credit facility, approximately 10% of revenue). Quarterly amortization at 2% of outstanding balance. No dividends — all earnings reinvested.
- **Scope:** All debt instruments.
- **Boundary:** Does not cover interest expense policy or debt covenants (see Explicit Gaps).

## 7. Tax

- **Policy:** Effective tax rate of 24%.
- **Scope:** All taxable income.
- **Boundary:** This is a simplified rate assumption. No policy for deferred tax, uncertain tax positions, or transfer pricing across regions (see Explicit Gaps).

---

## 8. Regional Operations

- **Policy:** Revenue and operations are distributed across four regions: AMER 45% ($2,250M), EMEA 30% ($1,500M), APAC 18% ($900M), LATAM 7% ($350M). Regional results are consolidated in USD.
- **Scope:** All revenue and operating metrics.
- **Boundary:** No foreign currency translation policy documented despite multi-region operations (see Explicit Gaps).

---

## 9. Headcount and People Costs

- **Policy:** Total headcount 30,000 (25,000 consultants + 5,000 corporate staff). Bench consultants: 4,500. Annual attrition rate: 15%. Corporate staff distributed across Sales (20%), Marketing (10%), Finance (8%), HR (5%), IT (12%), Legal (2.4%), G&A (40.6%). Meridian counts all personnel including contractors in headcount — this is a COFA conflict with Cascadia, which counts W-2 employees only.
- **Scope:** All personnel and related costs.
- **Boundary:** Stock-based compensation is not documented (see Explicit Gaps).

---

## 10. Intercompany Transactions

- **Policy:** Not applicable. Meridian is treated as a single consolidated entity for the purpose of this policy document.
- **Scope:** N/A.
- **Boundary:** Post-acquisition intercompany transactions with Cascadia will require a separate elimination policy.

## Explicit Gaps

The following accounting policy areas are not documented for Meridian and must be investigated before the agent may apply any treatment. Per Axiom 7 (No GAAP Inference), the absence of a policy means the agent must flag and halt — not guess.

| Gap | Reason | Risk if Unaddressed |
|-----|--------|---------------------|
| **Foreign currency translation** | Meridian operates in four regions (AMER/EMEA/APAC/LATAM) with material non-USD revenue, but no FX translation or remeasurement policy is documented. | Unreported FX gains/losses could misstate consolidated revenue and operating income. |
| **Goodwill impairment** | $2.4B goodwill from prior acquisitions. No impairment testing methodology, reporting unit definition, or triggers documented. | Overstated goodwill if reporting units underperform. |
| **Bad debt / credit losses** | No allowance for doubtful accounts policy despite $805M starting AR and 58-day DSO. | Overstated receivables if customers default. |
| **Stock-based compensation** | No SBC policy documented. A PE-backed consultancy with 30K employees likely has equity compensation. | Understated compensation expense if equity awards exist. |
| **Interest expense** | $500M credit facility but no interest rate or expense recognition policy. | Missing interest expense in P&L. |
| **Lease accounting** | No ASC 842 policy despite office facilities across multiple regions. | Potentially unrecognized ROU assets and lease liabilities. |
| **Deferred tax** | 24% effective rate is a simplification. No deferred tax asset/liability policy, no uncertain tax position policy, no transfer pricing policy for multi-region operations. | Materially misstated tax balances. |
| **Contingencies / litigation** | No policy for loss contingencies under ASC 450. | Undisclosed or unrecognized liabilities. |
